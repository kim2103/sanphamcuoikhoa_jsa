Baggage Tags: These are small numbered or encoded tags attached to baggage by the baggage handler or airline employee at the check-in counter. Each tag is unique to the bag and contains information such as the bag's destination, owner's name and contact information, and any special handling instructions. The baggage tag is scanned by an automated system to track the bags' movements through the airport and onto the aircraft.

Electronic Tags: These tags use radio frequency identification (RFID) technology to track and identify a bag's location. Unlike traditional baggage tags, RFID tags allow airport personnel to scan and track a bag's movements without the need for physical contact, making it easier and faster to locate lost or misrouted bags.

Self-Adhesive Tags: Some airlines allow passengers to print out their own baggage tags at home or at the airport kiosk. These tags can be attached to the luggage handle or elsewhere on the bag for easy identification.
